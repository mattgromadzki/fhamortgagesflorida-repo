FHA Home Loan Florida

What is an FHA loan in Florida?

An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration that makes homeownership more accessible. FHA loans in Florida are popular with first-time buyers because they allow smaller down payments and more forgiving credit guidelines than conventional financing — making them one of the easiest paths to buying a home in the Sunshine State.

Just 3.5% down

With a credit score of 580 or higher, you can buy a Florida home with a down payment of only 3.5% — and gift funds from family are allowed.

Flexible credit

FHA mortgages in Florida accept lower credit scores than most conventional loans. Scores from 500–579 may qualify with 10% down.

First-time buyer friendly

FHA pairs well with Florida down payment assistance programs, helping more buyers afford their first home statewide.

Mortgage Insurance

Understanding FHA MIP

FHA loans include a one-time upfront mortgage insurance premium plus an annual premium paid monthly.

1.75%

Upfront MIP — financed into your loan amount.

0.55%

Annual MIP* — paid monthly. With less than 10% down it stays for the life of the loan; with 10%+ down it drops after 11 years.

FHA by Buyer Situation

FHA loan options for every Florida buyer

Whatever your starting point, there's likely an FHA path that fits. These are general FHA guidelines — lender overlays and exceptions apply, so talk to us about your specifics.

FHA loans for first-time home buyers in Florida+
FHA is one of the most popular paths for first-time buyers in Florida: 3.5% down, gift funds allowed, and the option to pair it with state assistance like the Florida Hometown Heroes program. You don't have to be a first-time buyer to use FHA, but the low barriers make it especially appealing for one.
FHA loans with a 580 credit score+
A 580 FICO is the threshold for the 3.5% minimum down payment. Many Florida buyers in the 580–620 range choose FHA because qualifying is more attainable than conventional financing at the same score.
FHA loans with a 500–579 credit score+
Borrowers with scores between 500 and 579 may still qualify for an FHA loan with a 10% down payment, though individual lender overlays vary. We can review where you stand.
FHA loans after bankruptcy+
After a Chapter 7 bankruptcy there's generally a two-year waiting period before an FHA loan; after Chapter 13 you may qualify after about 12 months of on-time plan payments with court approval. Extenuating circumstances can shorten the wait.
FHA loans after foreclosure+
FHA typically requires a three-year waiting period after a foreclosure, measured from the completion date. Documented extenuating circumstances (such as a serious one-time event) may allow an exception.
FHA loans for self-employed borrowers+
Self-employed Florida buyers generally need about two years of documented self-employment income (tax returns and profit/loss statements). We help structure your file to present your income clearly.
FHA by Property Type

What you can buy with an FHA loan in Florida

FHA covers far more than single-family homes — including condos and small multi-unit properties you live in.

FHA loans for single-family homes+
The most common FHA scenario: a single-family primary residence with 3.5% down, subject to your county's FHA loan limit and an FHA appraisal.
FHA condo loans in Florida+
To finance a condo with FHA, the project usually needs to be FHA-approved, or you may use FHA's single-unit approval process for an individual unit. We'll check your building's status before you make an offer.
FHA loans for 2–4 unit properties+
FHA allows duplexes, triplexes, and fourplexes if you live in one unit as your primary residence. Loan limits are higher for multi-unit homes, and projected rental income may help you qualify.
FHA loans for manufactured homes+
Manufactured homes can be FHA-eligible when permanently affixed to a foundation and meeting HUD standards. Terms differ from site-built homes; ask us about specifics.

FHA Loans Florida FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is an FHA loan and how does it work in Florida?+
An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration. The government insurance lets lenders offer lower down payments and more flexible credit terms, which is why FHA is so popular with Florida first-time buyers. You apply through an FHA-approved lender like us.
What are the FHA loan requirements in Florida?+
Generally a 580+ credit score for 3.5% down (or 500–579 with 10% down), a debt-to-income ratio typically up to 43%–50%, steady verifiable income, and a primary residence that passes an FHA appraisal.
What credit score do I need for an FHA loan in Florida?+
580 or higher qualifies you for the 3.5% minimum down payment. Scores of 500–579 may qualify with 10% down, subject to lender overlays.
How much down payment do I need?+
As little as 3.5% with a 580+ score. Funds can come from savings or a gift from family, and sellers may contribute up to 6% toward closing costs.
What is the FHA loan limit in Florida for 2026?+
Single-family limits range from $541,287 in most counties up to $990,150 in Monroe County. Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach are above the $541,287 floor; Collier (Naples) is above the $541,287 floor.
Which Florida county has the highest FHA loan limit?+
Monroe County (the Florida Keys) at $990,150 for a single-family home in 2026.
Do FHA loan limits change every year?+
Yes. HUD adjusts them annually based on home prices. The 2026 floor rose to $541,287 from $524,225 in 2025.
Are FHA limits higher for multi-unit properties?+
Yes. In most Florida counties the 2026 limit is $541,287 for one unit and rises to $1,041,125 for a four-unit property.
What are current FHA mortgage rates in Florida?+
FHA rates change daily and depend on your credit, loan amount, down payment, term, and the market. They're often competitive with or lower than conventional rates. Request a personalized quote for your real rate.
Are FHA rates lower than conventional rates?+
FHA base rates are often lower, especially for lower credit scores, because federal insurance reduces lender risk. FHA also has mortgage insurance, so compare total cost side by side.
Do FHA loans require mortgage insurance?+
Yes — a 1.75% upfront premium (usually financed) and an annual premium around 0.55% paid monthly. With under 10% down it lasts the life of the loan; with 10%+ down it ends after 11 years.
Can I remove FHA mortgage insurance later?+
If you put less than 10% down, MIP stays for the loan's life, but many Florida homeowners refinance into a conventional loan once they reach about 20% equity to drop mortgage insurance.
Can first-time buyers use an FHA loan in Florida?+
Absolutely — it's one of the most popular first-time-buyer options thanks to the low down payment and flexible credit, and it pairs with Florida down payment assistance programs.
Can I get an FHA loan after bankruptcy?+
Usually two years after a Chapter 7 discharge, or about 12 months into a Chapter 13 with on-time payments and court approval. Extenuating circumstances may shorten the wait.
Can I get an FHA loan after a foreclosure?+
Generally three years after the foreclosure completes, with possible exceptions for documented extenuating circumstances.
Can self-employed borrowers get an FHA loan?+
Yes, typically with about two years of documented self-employment income via tax returns and profit/loss statements.
Can I buy a condo with an FHA loan in Florida?+
Yes, if the condo project is FHA-approved or qualifies under FHA's single-unit approval. We'll verify the building before you make an offer.
Can I buy a duplex or fourplex with an FHA loan?+
Yes — 2–4 unit properties are eligible if you occupy one unit as your primary residence, with higher loan limits and possible use of rental income to qualify.
What debt-to-income ratio do I need for FHA?+
FHA commonly allows DTI up to 43%–50% depending on compensating factors like cash reserves and credit strength.
Can I use gift funds for my FHA down payment?+
Yes. The entire 3.5% down payment can come from a gift from a family member, with proper documentation.
Does Florida offer down payment assistance with FHA loans?+
Yes. Programs such as Florida Hometown Heroes and other state/local assistance can often be combined with an FHA loan. Availability and funding change, so ask us what's currently offered.
How long does FHA pre-approval take in Florida?+
Often the same day once we review your basic financial details, so you can make competitive offers quickly.
Can I refinance an existing FHA loan?+
Yes. The FHA Streamline refinance can lower your rate with reduced documentation, and FHA cash-out refinances let you tap equity. We handle both.
What is an FHA 203(k) loan?+
An FHA 203(k) renovation loan lets you finance the purchase (or refinance) of a home plus the cost of eligible repairs or improvements in a single loan — useful for fixer-uppers in Florida.
Are there income limits for FHA loans?+
No. Unlike USDA loans, FHA has no maximum income limit — qualification is based on credit, debt-to-income, and the loan amount versus your county limit.
Does the home have to pass an inspection?+
FHA requires an appraisal that also checks the property meets minimum safety and livability standards. It's not the same as a full home inspection, which we still recommend.
What documents do I need to apply?+
Typically recent pay stubs, two years of W-2s or tax returns, two months of bank statements, photo ID, and details on your debts. Self-employed borrowers provide business returns.
Do you offer FHA loans throughout Florida?+
Yes — we serve buyers and homeowners in all 67 Florida counties, from Miami and Fort Lauderdale to Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville, Naples, and the Panhandle.
