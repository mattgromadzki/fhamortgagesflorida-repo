FHA Home Loan Florida

What is an FHA loan in Florida?

An FHA loan is a mortgage insured by the Federal Housing Administration that makes homeownership more accessible. FHA loans in Florida are popular with first-time buyers because they allow smaller down payments and more forgiving credit guidelines than conventional financing — making them one of the easiest paths to buying a home in the Sunshine State.

Just 3.5% down

With a credit score of 580 or higher, you can buy a Florida home with a down payment of only 3.5% — and gift funds from family are allowed.

Flexible credit

FHA mortgages in Florida accept lower credit scores than most conventional loans. Scores from 500–579 may qualify with 10% down.

First-time buyer friendly

FHA pairs well with Florida down payment assistance programs, helping more buyers afford their first home statewide.

FHA Loans Florida FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What are the FHA loan requirements in Florida?+
To qualify for an FHA loan in Florida you generally need a credit score of 580 or higher for 3.5% down (or 500–579 with 10% down), a debt-to-income ratio typically up to 43%–50%, steady verifiable income, and the property must be your primary residence and pass an FHA appraisal.
What is the FHA loan limit in Florida for 2026?+
For 2026, single-family FHA loan limits in Florida range from $541,287 in most counties up to $990,150 in Monroe County (the Florida Keys). Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach are above the $541,287 floor and Collier (Naples) is above the $541,287 floor. Limits are higher for 2–4 unit properties.
What are current FHA mortgage rates in Florida?+
FHA mortgage rates in Florida change daily and depend on your credit score, loan amount, down payment, term, and market conditions. FHA rates are frequently competitive with or lower than conventional rates. Request a free personalized quote to see your real rate today.
How much down payment do I need for an FHA loan in Florida?+
FHA loans in Florida require as little as 3.5% down with a credit score of 580 or higher. Your down payment can come from savings or a gift from a family member, and you may be able to combine it with Florida down payment assistance.
Can first-time home buyers in Florida use an FHA loan?+
Yes. FHA loans are one of the most popular options for first-time home buyers in Florida thanks to the low down payment, flexible credit guidelines, and the ability to pair FHA financing with state assistance programs.
Do I have to pay mortgage insurance on an FHA loan?+
Yes. FHA loans include an upfront mortgage insurance premium (1.75% of the loan, usually financed) and an annual premium paid monthly. With less than 10% down the annual premium stays for the life of the loan; with 10% or more down it ends after 11 years.
